<p>
Cotton tailed, you grazed on a hill overseeing lower pastures<br>
Gregarious, the herd has its velocity, rules for gathering<br>
Evening crackles as dirt gets moved for new burrows, dens<br>
Thumps from other dens, the blessed audition of messages heard<br>
</p>
<p>
Sun frayed grasslands cycle through horizons loaned freely<br>
Every burrow has a purpose, the empty ones will be reused<br>
Standing on top of the very hill you found yourself in, this<br>
Constitution begins a new trek across the tangible horizons<br>
</p>
<p>
Without demure, the scamp touches of tender feet scouts ahead<br>
Not looking at another glance of rising curious furred ears<br>
No signs of ground oppressors, through the rose thorns now<br>
By the furthers mile, your nose picks up on the cooling mist<br>
</p>
<p>
Once the hard soil of the grasslands, now the muddy shores<br>
Visions of a nearby hill of familiar hardness stood in front<br>
Cautiously you plight yourself on top of the mound of sticks<br>
And with a gentle unintentional push the hill starts moving<br>
</p>
<p>
Forest green palette now a fog bank with a blue reflective shade<br>
The mound turtles across the calm waters further away from land<br>
You stick your head up high to see what's coming across the river<br>
Be it land, sticks, mud - the ground can hold any trekker hare<br>
</p>
